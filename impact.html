Impact

By creating resources for the students and staff at Ralston Middle School on how to sort their waste, hundreds of students each year will be educated about the importance of knowing where their waste goes as well as being able to contribute to a zero waste community. Students will have the knowledge to sort waste into three categories and much of the waste they produce can now decompose back into the Earth. 

Learning how to sort waste at the middle school age is important because it allows them to become connected with and contribute to their community in a beneficial way. They become responsible and show respect for the environment through the action of sorting their waste and helping others learn. 

I hope my project inspires surrounding schools to take their own steps to implementing a tri-bin waste system. While most schools have trash and recycling, compost is a very important category to add due to the amount of leftover food students tend to have from their lunches. Compostable items cannot properly decompose in the landfills, so they end up producing methane, a greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change. It's important for us to do what we can to prevent global warming and allow plant based materials to decompose back into the Earth!
